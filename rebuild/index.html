The Fleet layer

Accountable capacity, not a black box.

Fleet owns the platform facts a founder needs before agent work can be trusted: who exists, which team they belong to, what accounts they inherit, whether they are running, and how usage rolls up.

01

Provisioned identity

Every managed agent has a scoped identity. Capacity is named, grouped, and auditable rather than appearing as an anonymous session.

02

Team-scoped access

Grant an account to a team. Agents added later inherit the same approved boundary without a new credential-copying ritual.

03

Runtime control

See active and paused capacity, then scale or stop it deliberately. Desired configuration and live runtime actions remain distinct.

04

Usage and receipts

Consumption rolls up at the team and account level. The platform record connects active compute to billing instead of hiding it in a service bundle.

The accountability chain

From account grant to receipt, one record.

  1. 01
    Declare the team

    Choose the agents and the capacity the account should own.

  2. 02
    Grant the boundary

    Attach approved repos, communication surfaces, and other accounts to the team.

  3. 03
    Operate the runtime

    Inspect status, pause, resume, or scale without changing the evidence trail.

  4. 04
    Reconcile usage

    Review team-level consumption and receipts against the capacity that ran.

No hidden chain of thought. No claim that autonomy alone is unique. The product is the accountable platform record around the agents.
